Q3 Results Summary

Telkom Indonesia reported Q3 2025 results that were largely in-line with expectations. Revenue grew 3.2% year-over-year, driven by Telkomsel's mobile data segment. Enterprise solutions showed some softness, which we attribute to competitive pressure and delayed capex decisions by corporate clients.

What's Working

Telkomsel continues to be the backbone of the business. ARPU stabilized after declining for several quarters, a positive sign that the market has found an equilibrium on data pricing. IndiHome, while facing some churn, remains profitable and generates meaningful free cash flow.

Our View

The 7.2% forward dividend yield creates a meaningful floor for the stock. Even if operational improvements are slow, investors are being paid to wait. We are maintaining our position and may add opportunistically below Rp 3,000 if the fundamentals remain intact.

At current valuations, the dividend alone compensates for near-term execution risk.

Watch for Next Quarter

  • Telkomsel 5G rollout progress and capex guidance
  • IndiHome subscriber trend — stabilization would be bullish
  • Enterprise segment order backlog

Conclusion

We are maintaining the position. TLKM is not a growth story at current prices — it is a yield story. The 18% portfolio allocation reflects this: meaningful but not overweight.
